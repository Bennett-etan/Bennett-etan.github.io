I recently graduated in May of 2016 from Stevens Institute of Technology with a Bachelor of Engineering in Electrical Engineering and a Master of Engineering in Systems Engineering. During my studies at Stevens, I held intern positions at The Aerospace Corporation, Maquet Cardiovascular, and General Motors in the electrical engineering field. I just began my career as a Systems Engineer at Harris Corporation in Clifton, NJ.

I succeeded in designing my first circuit board working in Germany for Maquet Cardiopulmonary after my previous internship with the company in 2014. At this final internship of my collegiate career, I was able to implement my knowledge of electrical systems, applied engineering methods, and systems development to prototype a medical device.

Working alongside operators, skilled tradesmen, senior engineers, technicians, fellow interns, and scientists I use knowledge gained at Stevens on every project I encounter. I have expanded my leadership skills communicating with colleagues in the workplace, while striving to act as a team. In several cases as an experienced intern, I trained my peers on lab equipment, and ensured that they understood the method of documenting testing processes.
